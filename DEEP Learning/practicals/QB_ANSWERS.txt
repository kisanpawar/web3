Deep Learning — DL_Practical_QB.pdf (concise answers; programs in this folder)

Practical 1 — Feed-Forward NN
Q1 (SGD vs Adam): Train both on MNIST with same architecture; plot loss/val_accuracy; report test accuracy. Typically Adam converges faster and reaches higher accuracy than plain SGD on this task.
Q2 (SGD vs RMSProp): Same procedure. RMSProp usually improves on SGD due to adaptive learning rates; compare curves and final test metrics.
Q3 (Adam vs RMSProp): Both are adaptive; Adam often edges RMSProp on MNIST FFN; justify with your logged test accuracy and convergence speed.

Practical 2 — Regularization
Q1 (L2): Baseline overfits (high train acc, lower val acc). L2 penalizes large weights, shrinking the train–val gap and often improving val/test accuracy.
Q2 (Dropout): Randomly drops units during training; reduces co-adaptation; val accuracy tracks closer to train than the unregularized model.

Practical 3 — Image classification
Q1 (CIFAR-10): CNN + pooling learns spatial features; report test accuracy; show predictions on unseen test images.
Q2 (MNIST CNN): Same pipeline on 28×28×1; typically very high test accuracy.
Q3 (Fashion-MNIST): Harder than MNIST; CNN still appropriate; report accuracy and sample predictions.

Practical 4 — Autoencoder
Q1 (MNIST): Encoder compresses to bottleneck; decoder reconstructs; measure/visualize reconstruction (MSE or binary cross-entropy loss on sigmoid outputs).
Q2 (CIFAR-10): Use convolutional encoder/decoder; reconstructions are blurrier than MNIST due to color/texture complexity.

Practical 5 — Digit recognition
Q1: Load/explore MNIST, normalize, FFN (Flatten + Dense + softmax), train with validation, predict and visualize a test digit; report test accuracy.

Practical 6 — Transfer learning
Q1: Load cats/dogs; freeze VGG16 body; train new classifier head; fewer epochs needed than from scratch; report validation accuracy.

Practical 7 — GAN
Q1 (MNIST): Generator maps noise → 784 tanh outputs; discriminator is binary classifier; alternating training; inspect synthetic digit grid.
Q2 (Fashion-MNIST): Same idea; synthetic images resemble clothing textures when training is sufficient.

Practical 8 — RNN
Q1 (Rainfall): Build (N,4,1) windows; SimpleRNN predicts next day; plot actual vs predicted on the sequence.
Q2 (Temperature): Same 4-to-1 RNN template on the temperature series.

Practical 9 — LSTM sentiment
Q9: Tokenize/pad text; Embedding + LSTM + sigmoid; binary cross-entropy; evaluate on held-out or test sentences; accuracy / qualitative examples.

Practical 10 — YOLOv3
Q1 (Image): Load cfg/weights/names; blob → forward → NMS → draw boxes; save/display result.
Q2 (Video): Per-frame same pipeline; optional video writer or save frames (headless: write PNG sequence).
